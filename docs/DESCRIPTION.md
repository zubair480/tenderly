# Tenderly — project description

## Elevator pitch

Tenderly is an AI volunteer-matching platform for San Francisco. A person uploads a resume, tells us the causes they care about and the time they can give, and receives three nonprofit opportunities where their skills can make an immediate difference—each with a specific, human explanation of why the match matters.

Unlike a generic volunteer directory, Tenderly responds to the city’s current needs. When a cold snap, service surge, or neighborhood trend changes local demand, the recommendations visibly re-rank so a volunteer can see where their help is most needed right now.

## The problem

People want to help but often meet three kinds of friction:

1. They do not know which local organization needs their particular skills.
2. Long, undifferentiated opportunity lists make action feel overwhelming.
3. The urgency behind a need is invisible, so volunteers cannot tell when a small commitment could matter most.

At the same time, nonprofits must explain needs repeatedly and compete for attention even as conditions change across the city.

## The solution

Tenderly turns a volunteer’s experience, interests, and availability into a short, explainable action plan. It combines:

- AI-assisted profile extraction from an uploaded resume.
- Skill, cause, availability, and neighborhood-aware opportunity matching.
- A current community-needs signal sourced from San Francisco open data.
- An AI-written `why you` explanation that makes every recommendation understandable.
- A live scenario control that demonstrates recommendations changing as community conditions change.

The result is not “here are 50 places to browse.” It is “here are the three places where your contribution can help now, and why.”

## Who it serves

**Primary user:** a San Francisco-area volunteer who wants a meaningful, practical way to help but has limited time and does not know where their skills fit.

**Secondary beneficiary:** local nonprofits that need better-fit volunteers and a way to surface urgency without requiring people to interpret raw public data.

## Why this is a strong social-good project

- It focuses on a local, actionable problem rather than a generic AI showcase.
- AI performs meaningful work: it turns unstructured experience into a profile and produces grounded, individualized explanations.
- Public data has a visible consequence: changing need conditions re-rank suggested actions.
- It keeps human agency intact: Tenderly recommends and explains; the volunteer decides.
- It makes accessibility a first-class part of access to civic participation.

## What we will demonstrate

1. Upload a sample resume and select causes plus availability.
2. Watch Tenderly build an understandable volunteer profile.
3. See three high-fit opportunities with match strength, commitment, urgency, neighborhood, and a personal explanation.
4. Trigger a simulated San Francisco cold snap and watch the cards re-order around changed community needs.
5. Show the community pulse that grounds the recommendation change.

## Product promise

**When someone has the willingness to help, Tenderly turns uncertainty into a clear next step.**
